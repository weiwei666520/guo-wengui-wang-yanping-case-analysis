# Guo Wengui & Wang Yanping Case Analysis  
## Hidden Delay in Motion to Dismiss (Doc 227)

This repository documents a procedural issue observed in:

Case: United States v. Guo Wengui (1:23-cr-00118-AT)

---

## Key Finding

In Document 227, defense counsel indicated an intention to challenge the superseding indictment.

However:

- No actual Motion to Dismiss was filed at that time  
- The issue was deferred into a future briefing schedule  
- The superseding indictment (ECF 215) remained unchallenged  

---

## Why This Matters

This creates a procedural effect:

> The constitutional challenge (Fifth Amendment issue) is not triggered immediately.

Instead:

- The indictment remains valid by default  
- The court does not review its constitutionality  
- The case continues forward without interruption  

---

## Core Insight

This is not a direct legal conclusion, but a procedural reality:

**It is not whether the issue exists —  
but when the issue is formally raised.**
